# Grill me  ·  "Grill me before the QBR"

Rehearsal. The brain becomes the hardest client, board member, or interviewer in the room and presses the owner with the questions they will actually face. Then it captures the gaps so the knowledge becomes theirs and the vault's.

## Steps

1. **Scope the grilling.** Ask what the event is and who the tough audience is (a specific client, a sceptical exec, an interview panel). Pull context from the relevant `Clients/`, `People/`, `Projects/` and recent `Meetings/`, plus open questions in `05-synthesis.md`.
2. **Grill, one question at a time.** Ask the sharp ones: the objection they are dreading, the number they might not have, the decision they have not justified. Stay in character as the tough audience. Push on weak answers once before moving on.
3. **Track the gaps.** Note every question the owner could not answer cleanly, or answered with something not in the vault.
4. **Debrief.** Summarize: what they handled well, and the gaps. For each gap, offer to (a) dig the vault for the answer, or (b) capture the owner's answer as new knowledge.
5. **Write, on confirmation only.** Park a debrief note under `Meetings/` or `Projects/` (frontmatter: `type: rehearsal`, four fields). Any new facts the owner confirms get written back to the right notes via the ingest flow, with `last_updated` bumped. **Do not write anything the owner has not confirmed.** Append one line to `04-log.md`.

## Guardrails

- This skill is a sparring partner, not a flatterer. Hard questions are the value.
- Confirm before writing: a rehearsal where the owner says "no, don't save that" produces zero writes.
- Use only what the vault and the owner supply; do not invent client facts to make the drill feel real.
