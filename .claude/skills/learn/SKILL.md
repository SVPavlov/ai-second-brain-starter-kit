# Learn  ·  "What did we learn?"

The reflective pass save-pattern waits for: instead of the owner noticing a pattern, the brain goes looking for them. Run it after an intense week, a finished engagement, or whenever the log is rich and Patterns/ is quiet.

## Steps

1. **Pick the window** (default: since the last learn run, else the last two weeks) and harvest the raw material: `04-log.md` for what happened, recent `Meetings/` and client notes for what was done, `05-synthesis.md` for what kept coming back as friction.
2. **Look for three kinds of lessons:**
   - *Something that worked more than once* → a pattern candidate (route it through the save-pattern flow: generalized, sanitized, into `Patterns/`).
   - *Something that went wrong or kept costing time* → an anti-pattern note in `Patterns/` ("avoid X when Y") — knowing what not to do travels just as well.
   - *Something about how the owner works* (always books calls in the morning, always forgets follow-ups on Fridays) → propose one line for `01-me.md`, owner confirms.
3. **Confirm before writing.** Present the candidate lessons in a short list; the owner says which are real (a thing that happened twice may still be coincidence — asks, never guesses). Only confirmed lessons get filed.
4. **Close the loop.** Catalog, wikilinks to the engagements the lessons came from, one line to `04-log.md`.

## Guardrails

- Lessons cite their evidence: which meetings/notes support this pattern? No evidence, no lesson.
- Two data points is a hypothesis, not a law; write candidates as "seems to work" until they survive a third occurrence.
- Patterns are sanitized by construction (the save-pattern rule applies here in full).
