# Capture idea  ·  "Idea: ..."

The lowest-friction habit in the kit: say it, it is kept, get back to work. Ideas die in heads and in random notes apps; here they land where the brain will resurface them.

## Steps

1. **Capture verbatim first**, polish never. Append to `Projects/ideas.md` (create it on first use, standard frontmatter): date, the idea in the owner's own words, and — only if obvious from context — a `[[wikilink]]` to the client, project or person it relates to.
2. **One clarifying question maximum**, and only when the idea is unusable without it. The whole point is speed; an interrogation kills the habit.
3. **Resurface, don't hoard.** When an idea relates to an existing client or project, also drop a one-line pointer in that hub so it surfaces during client-context briefings. Ideas older than 60 days get swept by synthesis-run into a "sluimerend" section — visible, not deleted.
4. **Log** one line to `04-log.md`.

## Guardrails

- Never respond with more text than the idea itself; "genoteerd, staat bij [[client]]" is a complete answer.
- Ideas are not tasks: no owner/date needed. The moment the owner wants ACTION, it goes to `03-tasks.md` with owner and date like everything else.
